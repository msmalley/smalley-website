Origin: Project Eden

It started as a weekend hackathon. Project Eden was an exploration into passwordless authentication — proving that crypto wallets could be created using WebAuthN and PassKeys without traditional passwords or seed phrases. MPC transaction signing, native account support across Bitcoin and EVM networks, cross-device compatibility.

The prototype was functional enough to demonstrate a fundamental insight: passkey-native infrastructure could meet institutional-grade security requirements without traditional custodial risk.

The Pivot

I co-founded Oviato and led the transition from an ordinal and inscription studio to a passkey-native wallet-as-a-service infrastructure provider. The Project Eden prototype became the technical foundation. Where Eden was a proof-of-concept, Oviato packaged it as reusable APIs and SDKs for third-party wallet builders.

  • Designed cryptographic framework and passkey/WebAuthN architecture
  • Built prototypes demonstrating WebAuthN viability for consumer-grade, non-custodial UX
  • Articulated complex infrastructure concepts to non-technical investors
  • Contributed to investor materials and technical due diligence process

Outcome

The technical work and pivot narrative directly secured DraperVC investment. Oviato demonstrates how a hackathon prototype can evolve into funded B2B infrastructure when the technical insight is sound and the articulation is clear.
